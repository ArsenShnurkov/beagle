{\vspace*{-3pc} Nemirovski Estimate of Mean of Arbitrary Distributions\\
with Known Variance.}\author{Leonid A.~Levin}\maketitle

\noindent Assume independent variables with the same mean $m$ and known
upper-bounds on variance.\\ We want to estimate the unknown $m$ with
accuracy $\e$ and a high chance of success.\\ We normalize the variables
 to set $\e=1$ and spread them into $2k-1$ groups\\ with $\le v\le
(\sqrt2-1)^2$ variance of averages $x_j$ of variables in each group $j$.\\
 By Chebyshev inequality, $x_j$ deviate from $m$
 to each side by $\ge 1$ with probability $\le v/(1+v)$.
 Their median then deviates from $m$ by $\ge1$ with probability
$P\le2\sum_{i=0}^{k-1}{2k-1\choose k+i} v^{k+i}/ (1+v)^{2k-1}$.
 [A.S.Nemirovsky, D.B.Yudin. {\em Problem Complexity and
  Method Efficiency in Optimization.} 1983.]

\section* {Estimating the Binomial Sum.}

$P(1+v)^{2k-1}/(2v^k)\le\sum_{i=0}^{k-1}{2k-1\choose k+i}v^i <$
${2k-1\choose k}\sum_{i=0}^{k-1}v^i<$ ${2k-1\choose k}/(1-v)$.
 Using $n!=(n/e)^n \sqrt{2\pi n+\theta}$, $1\!<\!\theta\!<\!1.05$,
 we estimate ${2k-1\choose k} <4^k/2\sqrt{k\pi}$ and $P<
 (4v/(1\!+\!v)^2)^k(1\!+\!v)/((1\!-\!v)\sqrt{k\pi})\le 2^{-k}\sqrt{2/k\pi}$.
